Where Every Spot Tells a Story

Ilocos Sur is filled with history, culture, and adventure. Explore its beauty below.

Candon City Eco Park: A Green Haven in Ilocos Sur

Candon City Eco Park in Ilocos Sur is a lush 395-hectare park that showcases the beauty of nature and local culture.
Visitors can enjoy hiking trails, gardens, a mini zoo, and picnic areas perfect for families and nature lovers.
The park also hosts cultural and environmental activities, making it a relaxing and educational destination for tourists.

#CandonCityEcoPark, #IlocosSurTourism, #NatureGetawayPH, #EcoTourismPhilippines, #FamilyFriendlyDestination

Sta. Maria Church: A Historic Gem of Ilocos Sur

Sta. Maria Church, or the Our Lady of the Assumption Parish Church, is a historic 18th-century church in Ilocos Sur.
Built on a hill, it offers stunning panoramic views of the town and surrounding countryside. The church is famous for its massive
adobe walls, intricate altar, and well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture.

#StaMariaChurch, #IlocosSurTourism, #HeritageSitePH, #SpanishColonialChurch, #TravelPhilippines

Santiago Cove: The Boracay of the North

Santiago Cove in Santiago, Ilocos Sur, is a serene white-sand beach often called the “Boracay of the North.”
Its clear turquoise waters are perfect for swimming, boating, and other water activities. The cove is also lined with
resorts and restaurants, making it a relaxing spot for both tourists and locals.

#SantiagoCove, #BoracayOfTheNorth, #IlocosSurBeaches, #TropicalParadisePH, #TravelIlocos
